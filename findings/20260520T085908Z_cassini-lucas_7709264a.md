# Cassini-Lucas identity at n=34

## Claim

`L(n+1) · L(n-1) − L(n)² = −5·(−1)^n`  (equivalently `5·(−1)^(n+1)`)

## Numerical check at n=34

- `L(33) = 7881196`
- `L(34)   = 12752043`
- `L(35) = 20633239`
- LHS = `L(35) · L(33) − L(34)²` = `20633239 · 7881196 − 12752043²` = **-5**
- RHS = `−5·(−1)^34` = **-5**
- Equal? **True**

## Notes

This is the Lucas analogue of Cassini's Fibonacci identity F(n+1)F(n-1) − F(n)² = (−1)^n.
The factor 5 emerges because L(n)² − 5·F(n)² = 4·(−1)^n, which couples the Lucas
square to the discriminant of x² − x − 1.
